- Provocative statements about AI-native development
- Challenge conventional wisdom
- Position as contrarian expert
- Format: Single bold statement or short thread (2-3 tweets)

```
Unpopular opinion: Teaching AI to read your docs is harder than teaching junior devs.
```

**Minimum viable consistency**: 1 post per day (7/week)
**Optimal**: 2 posts per day (14/week)

**Reply strategy** (15-20 minutes daily):
- Spend time replying to:
  - Anyone in the AI/dev tools space with <10K followers who posts about AI coding
  - Founders building in your niche (solo devs, AI agents, dev tools)

**Finding your people**:
- Search: "AI coding assistant", "AI agents", "context decay", "developer tools"
- Follow and engage with: Indie hackers building AI tools, VCs investing in dev tools, AI researchers working on code generation
- Build relationships BEFORE asking for anything

**DON'T**:
- Don't be promotional. Ever.
- Don't ask people to check out Kodebase
- Don't spam replies with generic comments

**DO**:
- Add genuine insight to others' threads
- Share your unique perspective
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Tag people when relevant (sparingly)

## Risks & Mitigation

**Risk 1: Time sink** (spending more time creating content than building)
- Mitigation: Set 45-minute daily timer, strictly enforce

**Risk 2: Audience mismatch** (vibe coders not on X/LinkedIn)
- Mitigation: After 60 days, pivot to Reddit/Discord if no traction

**Risk 3: Burnout** (consistency becomes overwhelming)
- Mitigation: Take 1 week breaks, batch content heavily

**Risk 4: Comparison trap** (watching others with 10K followers)
- Mitigation: Focus on quality of 100 engaged followers, not vanity metrics
